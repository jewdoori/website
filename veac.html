Motion & Velocity & Acceleration
运动 & 速度 & 加速度

Motion
运动

Motion is the act or process of changing place or position. An object may be in motion with respect to one object and motionless with respect to another. For example, a person sitting quietly in an aircraft flying at 200 knots is at rest or motionless with respect to the aircraft; however, the person and the aircraft are in motion with respect to the air and to the earth.

运动是改变位置的行为或过程。 一个物体可能相对于一个别的物体是运动的，同时相对于另一个物体是静止的。 比如，一个人静止坐在一架速度200节的飞机上，其相对于飞机人是静止的，然而，人和飞机相对于空气和地球来说是运动的。

Air has no force or power, except pressure, unless it is in motion. When it is moving, however, its force becomes apparent. A moving object in motionless air has a force exerted on it as a result of its own motion. It makes no difference in the effect then, whether an object is moving with respect to the air or the air is moving with respect to the object. The flow of air around an object caused by the movement of either the air or the object, or both, is called the relative wind.

除非空气正在运动，否则除了压力，不会产生力和功率的作用。 然而当空气正在运动时，其力的作用变得相当明显。一个在静止空气中运动的物体，由于其自身的运动， 会受到力的作用。无论是物体相对于空气运动，还是空气相对于物体运动，其产生的效果没有任何区别。 由物体的运动、空气的运动或两者同时的运动，使空气绕物体的流动，被称为相对气流。

Velocity
速度

The terms "speed" and "velocity" are often used interchangeably, but they do not have the same meaning. Speed is the rate of motion in relation to time, and velocity is the rate of motion in a particular direction in relation to time. That means, velocity is a vector quantity.

名词“速率”和“速度”通常相互替代使用，但它们意思不同。速率是相对于时间的运动速率，而速度是 朝着特定方向，相对于时间的运动速率。这意味着，速度是矢量。

An aircraft starts from New York City and flies 10 hours at an average speed of 260 miles per hour (mph). At the end of this time, the aircraft may be over the Atlantic Ocean, Pacific Ocean, Gulf of Mexico, or, if its flight were in a circular path, it may even be back over New York City. If this same aircraft flew at a velocity of 260 mph in a southwestward direction, it would arrive in Los Angeles in about 10 hours. Only the rate of motion is indicated in the first example and denotes the speed of the aircraft. In the last example, the particular direction is included with the rate of motion, thus, denoting the velocity of the aircraft.

一架飞机从纽约起飞，以260英里每小时（mph）的速率飞行10小时。时间结束后，飞机可能在大西洋上，太平洋上，墨西哥湾上， 或者，如果飞机按圆弧飞行，甚至有可能飞回纽约市。如果同一架飞机，以260 mph的速度向西南方向飞行，大约10小时后飞机会飞到洛杉矶。 在第一个例子中，只表示了飞机速率。在第二个例子中，包含运动速率和具体的方向，以这种方式表示飞机速度。

Acceleration
加速度

Acceleration is defined as the rate of change of velocity. An aircraft increasing in velocity is an example of positive acceleration, while another aircraft reducing its velocity is an example of negative acceleration, or deceleration.

加速度的定义是速度的变化率，一个速度正在增加的飞机有正加速度，一个速度正在减小的飞机有负加速度，或称减速度。
