Air pressure
空气压力

Pressure is the force applied in a perpendicular direction to the surface of an object. Often, pressure is measured in pounds of force exerted per square inch of an object——PSI(America). There are also some other ways of measuring pressure, we will talk about that later. An object completely immersed in a fluid will feel pressure uniformly around the entire surface of the object. If the pressure on one surface of the object becomes less than the pressure exerted on the other surfaces, the object will move in the direction of the lower pressure.

压力是垂直于物体表面施加的力，通常，压力用施加在物体每“平方英寸”上的压力“磅”——PSI表示（美制单位）。还有其他表示压力的单位，我们将会在之后提到。 一个完全浸入流体的物体的所有表面将会一致地承受压力。如果一侧表面的压力变得小于另一侧的压力， 物体会朝着低压的方向运动。

Amontons's Law：Given a constant amount of a gas and an unchanged volume, pressure is directly proportional to temperature. That's because when the gas temp goes up, kinetic energy rises, making the gas tend to expand. Due to the unchanged volume, there is no more space for the expanded gas. Thus, increases in gas pressure.

阿蒙顿定律：固定体积的一定量的气体，其压强与温度呈正比。 这是因为当气体温度升高时使气体膨胀。由于体积固定，没有更多空间给气体进行膨胀，造成压力升高。

Atmospheric pressure
大气压力

The air in the earth’s atmosphere is composed mostly of nitrogen and oxygen. Air is considered a fluid because it fits the definition of a substance that has the ability to flow or assume the shape of the container in which it is enclosed. If the container is heated, pressure increases; if cooled, the pressure decreases. The density of air is greatest at sea level where it has been compressed by all of the air above. This compression of air is called atmospheric pressure.

地球的大气主要由氮气和氧气组成。空气符合能够流入容器并展现出容器的形状的物质的定义，所以被认为是流体。 如果加热这个容器，压力上升，若是冷却，则压力降低。在海平面处的空气密度最大，因为这里的空气被上面的空气 压缩，这种对空气的压缩被称作大气压力。

Atmospheric pressure is usually defined as the force exerted against the earth’s surface by the weight of the air above that surface. Weight is force applied to an area that results in pressure. A column of air (one square inch) extending from sea level to the top of the atmosphere weighs approximately 14.7 pounds; therefore, atmospheric pressure can be stated in pounds per square inch (psi). Thus, atmospheric pressure at sea level is 14.7 psi.

大气压力通常定义为地球表面以上的空气的重量的力作用在地球表面，重量的力作用在一定区域，产生压力。 从海平面到大气层顶端，一个柱状体（底面积1平方英寸）范围内的空气重量大约是14.7磅，大气压力可用磅每平方英寸（PSI）表示， 因此，海平面处的大气压力是14.7PSI。

Atmospheric pressure is measured with an instrument called a barometer, composed of mercury in a tube that records atmospheric pressure in inches of mercury ("Hg). It's an inverted tube of mercury into a dish filled with the metal and observed that some of the mercury did not flow out of the tube, while the space above the mercury, in the closed top end of the tube, was a vacuum. The resultant mercury column was 76 cm (29.92 inches) tall. [Figure 1-1] The standard measurement in aviation altimeters and U.S. weather reports has been "Hg. However, worldwide weather maps and some non-U.S. manufactured aircraft instruments indicate pressure in millibars (mb)(now hectopascals(hpa)), a metric unit. At sea level, when the average atmospheric pressure is 14.7 psi, the barometric pressure is 29.92 "Hg, and the metric measurement is 1013.25 mb(hpa).

大气压力用一种叫做气压计的仪器测量，由一个装着水银，能够以英寸汞柱（缩写"Hg）为单位，测量大气压力的管子构成。气压计是将装满水银的试管倒置在装满水银的盘中，能够观测到一部分水银没有流出试管，在水银上面，试管的密封端产生真空。最终水银柱的高度是76厘米（29.92英寸） [图1-1]。 民航高度表和美国气象报告使用英寸汞柱作为气压测量标准，然而，世界各地的气象地图，以及一些美国之外的地区，生产的飞机仪表指示压力使用公制单位毫巴（mb）(现称百帕斯卡（hpa）)。 在海平面高度，当平均大气压是14.7PSI时，气压指示为29.92英寸汞柱，公制单位下为1013.25毫巴(百帕)。

An important consideration is that atmospheric pressure varies with altitude. As an aircraft ascends, atmospheric pressure drops, oxygen content of the air decreases, and temperature drops. The changes in altitude affect an aircraft’s performance in such areas as lift and engine horsepower. The effects of temperature, altitude, and density of air on aircraft performance are covered in the following chapters.

大气压力随高度的变化也是重要考虑因素。在飞机上升时，大气压力减小，空气氧含量降低，温度降低。 高度的变化影响诸如升力和发动机功率等飞机性能。温度，高度，大气密度对飞机性能的影响将在后面章节提到。
