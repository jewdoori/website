The Earth’s Atmosphere
地球大气

The Earth’s atmosphere is a cloud of gas and suspended solids extending from the surface out many thousands of miles, becoming thinner with distance, but always held by the Earth’s gravitational pull. The atmosphere is made up of layers surrounding the Earth that holds the air that people breathe, protects us from outer space, and holds moisture (e.g., vapor, clouds, and precipitation), gases, and tiny particles. In short, the atmosphere is the protective bubble that people live in.

地球大气由其中充斥着的气体和悬浮的固体构成，从地球表面向外延伸数千英里，随着距离边远逐渐变稀薄，被地球 重力约束着。大气可分为数个层围绕着地球，为人类提供可以呼吸的空气，保护人类免受外太空的影响，并且容纳水分， （例如：水汽，云和降水），气体和微小的颗粒。总而言之，大气可以看做是人类生活的保护罩。

Composition
构成

The Earth’s atmosphere consists of numerous gases (see Table 1-1) with nitrogen, oxygen, argon, and carbon dioxide making up 99.998 percent of all gases. Nitrogen, by far the most common, dilutes oxygen and prevents rapid burning at the Earth’s surface. Living things need it to make proteins. Oxygen is used by all living things and is essential for respiration. Plants use carbon dioxide to make oxygen. Carbon dioxide also acts as a blanket and prevents the escape of heat to outer space.

地球大气由数种气体构成（如图1-1）。氮气，氧气，氩气和二氧化碳占总气体的99.998％。氮气量最多， 氮气稀释了氧气，限制了地球表面的燃烧速度。生物需要氧气来产生蛋白质。氧气是呼吸的重要一环，所有的生物都需要氧气

Weather (the state of the atmosphere at any given time and place) strongly influences daily routine as well as general life patterns. Virtually all of our activities are affected by weather, but, of all of our endeavors, perhaps none more so than aviation.

气象（一定时间和地点大气的状态）对于生命周期和日常活动影响重大。几乎所有的人类活动都受到天气的影响，但是，在如此 多的人类活动中，或许没有一个活动比民用航空受到的影响还要大。

Air Parcel
气团

An air parcel is an imaginary volume of air to which any or all of the basic properties of atmospheric air may be assigned. A parcel is large enough to contain a very large number of molecules, but small enough so that the properties assigned to it are approximately uniform. It is not given precise numerical definition, but a cubic centimeter of air might fit well into most contexts where air parcels are discussed. In meteorology, an air parcel is used as a tool to describe certain atmospheric processes, and air parcels will be referred to in this and following chapters.

气团是一团假象的空气，可以体现大气的部分或全部基本属性。气团应当大到能够容纳大量的分子，也要小到使其内部性质近似相同。 气团不是一个精确的数字概念，但一立方厘米大小的空气也可能符合刚刚讲述气团所提到的大部分内容。在气象学中， 气团是一种用于描述特定的大气现象的工具，在本章和后续章节将会提到气团。

Vertical Structure
垂直结构

The Earth’s atmosphere is subdivided into five concentric layers (see Figure 1-2) based on the vertical profile of average air temperature changes, chemical composition, movement, and density. Each of the five layers is topped by a pause, where the maximum changes in thermal characteristics, chemical composition, movement, and density occur.

地球大气基于垂直剖面的平均温度变化，化学成分，运动和密度，分为五个圆心相同的层（如图1-2） 这五层每一层上面都有一个顶，在顶部，热性质、化学成分、运动和密度的变化达到最大。

Troposphere
对流层

The troposphere begins at the Earth’s surface and extends up to about 11 kilometers (km) (36,000 ft) high. This is where people live. As the gases in this layer decrease with height, the air becomes thinner. Therefore, the temperature in the troposphere also decreases with height. Climbing higher, the temperature drops from about 15 degrees Celsius (°C) (59 degrees Fahrenheit (°F)) to -56.5 °C (-70 °F). Almost all weather occurs in this region.

对流层从地标开始，延伸到大约11公里（km）（36000英尺）的高度。人类在对流层生活， 对流层高度增加气体减少，空气变得稀薄。因此，对流层的温度也随高度增加而降低。高度增加， 温度从大约15摄氏度（°C）（59华氏度）降低到-56.5°C（-70 °F）。几乎所有的天气现象 都发生在此范围内。

The vertical depth of the troposphere varies due to temperature variations, which are closely associated with latitude and season. It decreases from the Equator to the poles, and is higher during summer than in winter. At the Equator, it is around 18–20 km (11–12 miles (mi)) high, at 50° N and 50° S latitude, 9 km (5.6 mi), and at the poles, 6 km (3.7 mi) high.

对流层的高度随温度变化而变化，与纬度和季节紧密相关。对流层高度从赤道到极点逐渐降低， 夏天高，冬天低。在赤道，对流层大于18-20公里（11-12英里）高，在北纬和南纬50度，大约9公里（5.6英里）高， 而在极点，大约6公里（3.7英里）高。

The lowest portion of the troposphere is known as the planetary boundary layer. The height of the boundary layer varies depending on terrain and time of day, and is directly affected by surface heating and cooling. It has an important role in transporting heat and moisture into the atmosphere.

对流层的最底部叫做大气边界层（摩擦层），此边界层的高度根据地形和一天中时间的变化而变化。 并且被地表的升温和降温直接影响。其对于传递热能和水汽进入大气的过程至关重要。

The transition boundary between the troposphere and the layer above is called the tropopause. Both the tropopause and the troposphere are known as the lower atmosphere.

对流层与其上的气层中间的过渡层叫做对流层顶，对流层顶和对流层被称作低层大气。

Stratosphere
平流层

The stratosphere extends from the tropopause up to 50 km (31 mi) above the Earth’s surface. This layer holds 19 percent of the atmosphere’s gases, but very little water vapor.

平流层从对流层顶延伸到距离地球表面50公里（31英里）的高度。平流层包含大气总气体的19%，水汽含量很低。

Temperature increases with height as radiation is increasingly absorbed by oxygen molecules, leading to the formation of ozone. The temperature rises from an average -56.6 °C (-70 °F) at the tropopause to a maximum of about -3 °C (27 °F) at the stratopause due to this absorption of ultraviolet radiation. The increasing temperature also makes it a calm layer, with movements of the gases being slow.

因为高度增加，氧气分子吸收的辐射增加，温度升高，产生臭氧。因为紫外线的吸收，温度从对流层顶到平流层顶，平均温度-56.6 °C (-70 °F)升高到最大大约 -3 °C (27 °F)。温度随高度的升高使其成为一个稳定层，气体的运动减弱。

Commercial aircraft often cruise in the lower stratosphere to avoid atmospheric turbulence and convection in the troposphere. Severe turbulence during the cruise phase of flight can be caused by the convective overshoot of thunderstorms from the troposphere below. The disadvantages of flying in the stratosphere can include increased fuel consumption due to warmer temperatures, increased levels of radiation, and increased concentration of ozone.

商用飞机通常在平流层低层巡航以避免对流层中的大气湍流和对流。其下对流层中的对流雷暴可能突破到上层，造成强烈的湍流。 在平流层中飞行有些许缺点，包括由于温度增加导致油耗升高，辐射增强，以及臭氧浓度增加。

Mesosphere
中间层

The mesosphere extends from the stratopause to about 85 km (53 mi) above the Earth. The gases, including the number of oxygen molecules, continue to become thinner and thinner with height. As such, the effect of the warming by ultraviolet radiation also becomes less and less pronounced, leading to a decrease in temperature with height. On average, temperature decreases from about -3 °C (27 °F) to as low as -100 °C (-148 °F) at the mesopause. However, the gases in the mesosphere are thick enough to slow down meteorites hurtling into the atmosphere, where they burn up, leaving fiery trails in the night sky.

中间层从平流层顶延伸到距离地球表面大约85公里（53英里）的高度。 所有气体，包括氧气分子的数量，随着高度增加而逐渐变得稀薄。因此， 紫外线辐射导致的升温效果也逐渐减弱，使温度随高度升高而降低。 中间层温度大致从-3 °C (27 °F)降低至-100 °C (-148 °F)。 然而，中间层的大气浓度足以减慢陨石坠向地面的速度，使其燃烧并在夜空中留下燃烧尾迹。

Thermosphere
热层

The thermosphere extends from the mesopause to 690 km (430 mi) above the Earth. This layer is known as the upper atmosphere.

热层从中间层顶延伸到距离地球表面大约690公里（430英里）的高度。这一层又被称作高层大气。

The gases of the thermosphere become increasingly thin compared to the mesosphere. As such, only the higher energy ultraviolet and x ray radiation from the Sun is absorbed. But because of this absorption, the temperature increases with height and can reach as high as 2,000 °C (3,600 °F) near the top of this layer.

热层中的气体比起中间层变得更加稀薄。因此，只有来自太阳的能量更高的紫外线和X射线辐射才能被吸收。 正是由于辐射吸收，温度随着高度升高而增大，接近热层顶部时，温度能增大到接近2,000 °C (3,600 °F)

Despite the high temperature, this layer of the atmosphere would still feel very cold to our skin because of the extremely thin air. The total amount of energy from the very few molecules in this layer is not sufficient enough to heat our skin.

尽管温度很高，但由于热层的空气极其稀薄，我们皮肤仍然会感到十分寒冷，热层稀薄的分子所含有的总能量不足以 加热我们的皮肤。

Exosphere
逃逸层

The exosphere is the outermost layer of the atmosphere and extends from the thermopause to 10,000 km (6,200 mi) above the Earth. In this layer, atoms and molecules escape into space and satellites orbit the Earth. The transition boundary that separates the exosphere from the thermosphere is called the thermopause.

逃逸层是大气的最外层，其从热层顶延伸至距离地面10000千米（6200英里）的高度。在这一层， 原子和分子逃逸到太空，卫星在此围绕地球旋转，分隔逃逸层和热层的过渡层叫做热层顶。

The Standard Atmosphere
标准大气

Continuous fluctuations of atmospheric properties create problems for engineers and meteorologists who need a fixed standard for reference. To solve this problem, they defined a standard atmosphere, which represents an average of conditions throughout the atmosphere for all latitudes, seasons, and altitudes.

大气性质的持续波动对于需要一个固定的标准或参考的工程师和气象学家们造成了不小的麻烦。为了解决这一问题， 他们制定了标准大气，其代表了在任何纬度，季节，和高度下整个大气的条件的整体情况。

Standard atmosphere is a hypothetical vertical distribution of atmospheric temperature, pressure, and density that, by international agreement, is taken to be representative of the atmosphere for purposes of pressure altimeter calibrations, aircraft performance calculations, aircraft and missile design, ballistic tables, etc. (see Table 1-3 and Figure 1-4). Weather-related processes are generally referenced to the standard atmosphere, as are the examples in following chapters.

标准大气是假定的气温、气压和密度的垂直分布。根据国际协议，标准大气在气压式高度表调定，飞机性能计算， 飞机和火箭任务，弹道表，等等领域中代表一般大气。（如表格1-3和图1-4）。有关气象的工作一般都参考标准大气，在后面的章节中有许多例子。
