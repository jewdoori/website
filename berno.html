Bernoulli’s Principle
伯努利定律

Continuity principle
连续性定律

Bernoulli’s principle-continuity principle states that when a fluid (air) flowing through a tube reaches a constriction, or narrowing, of the tube, the speed of the fluid flowing through that constriction is increased and its pressure is decreased.

伯努利定律中连续性定律指的是，当流过管道的流体（空气）到达一个管道收缩处，或狭长处，流过收缩处的流体流动的速率增大，压力减小。

A practical application of Bernoulli’s Principle is the venturi tube. The venturi tube has an air inlet that narrows to a throat (the most constricted point) and an outlet section that increases in diameter toward the rear. The diameter of the outlet is the same as that of the inlet. The mass of air entering the tube must exactly equal the mass exiting the tube (law of conservation of mass). At the constriction, the speed must increase to allow the same amount of air to pass in the same amount of time as in all other parts of the tube. When the air speeds up, the pressure also decreases. Past the constriction, the airflow slows and the pressure increases. [Figure 1-1]

伯努利定律实际应用于文氏管。文氏管的进气口收缩到喉部（收缩最窄的点），出口向后逐渐增大直径， 出口的直径与进口直径相同。进入管道的空气质量与流出管道的空气质量相同（质量守恒定律）。 在收缩处，流速必须增加才能使相同量的空气在与流过管道其他部位时相同的时间内通过。 当空气流速增大，压力减小。流过收缩处后，气流减速，压力增大。[图1-1]
