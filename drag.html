Drag
阻力

Drag is the force that resists movement of an aircraft through the air. There are two basic types: parasite drag and induced drag. The first is called parasite because it in no way functions to aid flight, while the second, induced drag, is a result of an airfoil developing lift.

阻力是阻碍飞机在空气中运动的力。阻力分为两种：寄生阻力和诱导阻力。 第一种阻力被称为寄生阻力是因为他不会对飞行产生任何积极的影响。 而第二种，诱导阻力，是随翼型产生升力的而产生的。

Parasite Drag
寄生阻力（废阻力）

Parasite drag is comprised of all the forces that work to slow an aircraft’s movement. As the term parasite implies, it is the drag that is not associated with the production of lift. This includes the displacement of the air by the aircraft, turbulence generated in the airstream, or a hindrance of air moving over the surface of the aircraft and airfoil. There are three types of parasite drag: form drag, interference drag, and skin friction.

寄生阻力由所有作用于飞机使飞机运动减慢的力构成。 当使用寄生这个词时，代表这部分阻力与升力的产生无关 。寄生阻力的产生来自飞机分离空气、由气流产生的湍流、 或是气流流过飞机翼型表面时受到的阻碍。寄生阻力一共有 三种类型：压差阻力，诱导阻力，摩擦阻力。

Form drag
压差阻力

Form drag is the portion of parasite drag generated by the aircraft due to its shape and airflow around it. Examples include the engine cowlings, antennas, and the aerodynamic shape of other components. When the air has to separate to move around a moving aircraft and its components, it eventually rejoins after passing the body. How quickly and smoothly it rejoins is representative of the resistance that it creates, which requires additional force to overcome. [Figure 1-1]

压差阻力是寄生阻力的一部分，是由飞机的形状和围绕飞机的气流产生的。 例如引擎整流罩，天线，和其他部件的气动外形。当空气由于飞机及其部件的移动而被迫 分离围绕飞机流动时，这些空气最终在流过机体后重新汇聚。气流汇聚的快慢和平滑程度决定 了其产生的阻力，这些阻力需要额外的力去抵消。[图 1-1]

In the upper figure of the figure 1-1, the relative wind across a flat plate results in a leading edge stagnation point at the front of the plate that contains very high pressure. The airflow attempts to maintain contact with the surface of the plate, but the streamlines are unable to follow the sharp angles which would be required to allow them to fill in behind the plate. As a result, they separate at the trailing edge of the plate leaving a low pressure wake area behind it. The pressure differential between the leading and trailing edges of the plate causes the plate to be pushed in the direction of the relative wind and retards forward motion. This is form drag. To reduce form drag, aircraft surfaces which are exposed to the airflow of the relative wind are streamlined. The remaining figures of the adjacent diagram show how, as the streamlining is increased, the form drag is decreased.

图1-1中最上面的图所示，相对气流流过平板时，在平板前端的驻点能够产生很大的压力。 气流有保持与物体表面相贴的趋势，但是流线无法转过那么大的角度去填补平板后面的空气。 因此，气流在后缘分离，并在平板后方产生一个低压的涡流区。前缘和后缘的压力差导致平板受到 向相对气流的方向的力，并减弱向前的运动趋势。这便是压差阻力。 为了减少压差阻力，飞机接触相对气流的表面都是流线型的，如其他相邻的图所示，当物体更加流线型时， 压差阻力减小。

Interference drag
干扰阻力

Interference drag comes from the intersection of airstreams that creates eddy currents, turbulence, or restricts smooth airflow. For example, the intersection of the wing and the fuselage at the wing root has significant interference drag. Air flowing around the fuselage collides with air flowing over the wing, merging into a current of air different from the two original currents. The most interference drag is observed when two surfaces meet at perpendicular angles. Fairings are used to reduce this tendency. If a jet fighter carries two identical wing tanks, the overall drag is greater than the sum of the individual tanks because both of these create and generate interference drag. Fairings and distance between lifting surfaces and external components (such as radar antennas hung from wings) reduce interference drag. [Figure 1-2]

干扰阻力来自气流交汇处对产生的涡流、湍流或交汇处对平滑气流的阻碍。 例如，机翼翼根与机身的连接处会产生严重的干扰阻力。 沿机身流动的气流与沿机翼流动的气流相遇，合并成一股与前两种 气流不同的气流。两个平面以直角连接时会产生强劲的干扰阻力。 可使用整流罩来减弱这种趋势。如果一架战斗机携带有两个相同的 机翼油箱，飞机上的两个油箱所产生的阻力会比两个油箱单独产生的阻力大， 这是因为产生了干扰阻力。 使用整流罩，保持外部部件与产生升力的平面和其他外部结构保持一定距离（例如雷达天线悬挂在机翼上） 可以减小干扰阻力。[图 1-2]

Skin friction drag
摩擦阻力

Skin friction drag is the aerodynamic resistance due to the contact of moving air with the surface of an aircraft. Every surface, no matter how apparently smooth, has a rough, ragged surface when viewed under a microscope. Friction Drag is created in the boundary layer due to the viscosity of the air and the resulting friction against the surface of the aircraft. The air molecules in direct contact with the aircraft surface are most affected. As the molecules flow past the surface and past each other, the viscous resistance to that flow becomes a force which retards forward motion. The amount of friction drag that is created per square metre of surface area is relatively small. However, as the boundary layer covers much of the surface of the aircraft, friction drag can become quite significant in larger aeroplanes.

摩擦阻力产生于运动的空气与飞机表面的接触。所有的表面无论看起来多光滑，在显微镜下都是粗糙崎岖的。 摩擦阻力随附面层产生，这是因为空气具有粘性，而空气粘性导致导致飞机表面产生摩擦。与飞机表面直接 接触的空气分子受到的影响最大，当空气分子流过表面和其他空气分子时，粘性对流动的阻碍形成了一种阻碍 向前运动的力。平均每平方米的表面产生的摩擦阻力相对较小，但是，由于附面层覆盖在飞机的大部分表面， 摩擦阻力对大飞机的影响很重要。

Turbulent flow creates more friction drag than laminar flow due to its greater interaction with the surface of the airplane. Rough surfaces accelerate the transition of boundary layer airflow from laminar to turbulent which, in turn, increases the thickness of and the airflow disruption within the boundary layer. These increases result in more air molecules being affected by the movement of the aircraft and a corresponding increase in friction drag.

In order to reduce the effect of skin friction drag, aircraft designers utilize flush mount rivets and remove any irregularities that may protrude above the wing surface. In addition, a smooth and glossy finish aids in transition of air across the surface of the wing. Since dirt on an aircraft disrupts the free flow of air and increases drag, keep the surfaces of an aircraft clean and waxed.

为了减小摩擦阻力的影响，飞机设计师运用嵌入式安装的铆钉，并去除机翼表面所有不规则的凸起。并且，光滑平整的加工有助于 空气流过机翼表面。因为飞机表面的灰尘会影响空气的流动，使阻力增大，所以应保持飞机表面清洁并上蜡。

Induced Drag
诱导阻力

The second basic type of drag is induced drag. It is an established physical fact that no system that does work in the mechanical sense can be 100 percent efficient. This means that whatever the nature of the system, the required work is obtained at the expense of certain additional work that is dissipated or lost in the system. The more efficient the system, the smaller this loss.

第二类阻力是诱导阻力。诱导阻力基于一个已有的物理规律：不存在某种系统能使机械意义上的效率达到100%。 这意味着无论这个系统本质是什么，获得所需的功的同时，会有一定量的功在系统中消散或丢失。效率越高的系统 ，丢失的功越少。

In level flight, the aerodynamic properties of a wing or rotor produce a required lift, but this can be obtained only at the expense of a certain penalty. The name given to this penalty is induced drag. Induced drag is inherent whenever an airfoil is producing lift and, in fact, this type of drag is inseparable from the production of lift. Consequently, it is always present if lift is produced.

在平飞过程中，由机翼或螺旋桨的气动特性产生升力，与此同时会产生一定的损失，这种损失被命名为诱导阻力。只要翼型产生升力 ，诱导阻力就一直存在，诱导阻力与升力的产生有着紧密联系，因此，诱导阻力与升力同时存在。

An airfoil (wing or rotor blade) produces the lift force by making use of the energy of the free airstream. Whenever an airfoil is producing lift, the pressure on the lower surface of it is greater than that on the upper surface (Bernoulli’s Principle). As a result, the air tends to flow from the high pressure area below the tip upward to the low pressure area on the upper surface. In the vicinity of the tips, there is a tendency for these pressures to equalize, resulting in a lateral flow outward from the underside to the upper surface. This lateral flow imparts a rotational velocity to the air at the tips, creating vortices that trail behind the airfoil.

翼型（机翼或螺旋桨叶）通过利用自由气流中的能量产生升力。每当翼型产生升力时， 下表面的压力大于上表面压力（伯努利定律）。因此，空气趋于从高压区域流向低压区域， 在翼尖附近有这种平衡压力的趋势，产生从下表面向上表面横向流动的气流。 这种横向流动的气流使翼尖处的空气产生旋转，在翼型后方产生涡流。

When the aircraft is viewed from the tail, these vortices circulate counterclockwise about the right tip and clockwise about the left tip. [Figure 1-3] As the air (and vortices) roll off the back of your wing, they angle down, which is known as downwash. How the downwash generates has been discussed further in the chapter "Wing-tip vortices". Figure 1-4 shows the difference in downwash at altitude versus near the ground. Bearing in mind the direction of rotation of these vortices, it can be seen that they induce an upward flow of air beyond the tip and a downwash flow behind the wing’s trailing edge. This induced downwash has nothing in common with the downwash that is necessary to produce lift. It is, in fact, the source of induced drag.

当从飞机的后方向前看时，绕右侧翼尖旋转的涡流逆时针旋转，绕左侧翼尖旋转的涡流顺时针旋转。[图1-3] 当空气（和涡流）流过机翼，这些气流会向下偏转，被称作下洗流。下洗流的产生在“翼尖涡流”章节中详细讲到。图1-4展示了在空中和在近地面区域时，下洗流的区别。 记住这些涡流的旋转方向，可以得出在翼尖前缘部分的气流向上运动，在机翼后缘部分的气流向下运动， 这种下洗流与机翼产生升力而产生的下洗流不同，这种下洗流是诱导阻力产生的真正原因。

Downwash points the relative wind downward, so the more downwash you have, the more your relative wind points downward. That's important for one very good reason: lift is always perpendicular to the relative wind. In Figure 1-5, you can see that when you have less downwash, your lift vector is more vertical, opposing gravity. And when you have more downwash, your lift vector points back more, causing induced drag. On top of that, it takes energy for your wings to create downwash and vortices, and that energy creates drag.

下洗流使相对气流向下偏转，所以下洗流越大，相对气流向下偏转的越多。因为升力总是垂直于相对气流，因此下洗流非常重要。 图1-5， 你可以看到，当下洗流越小，升力矢量就越垂直，与重力相抗衡。当下洗流越大时，升力矢量向后倾斜， 产生诱导阻力。或者说，机翼产生下洗流和涡流需要消耗能量，而消耗能量导致了阻力的产生。

图1-5

The greater the size and strength of the vortices and consequent downwash component on the net airflow over the airfoil, the greater the induced drag effect becomes. This downwash over the top of the airfoil at the tip has the same effect as bending the lift vector rearward; therefore, the lift is slightly aft of perpendicular to the relative wind, creating a rearward lift component. This is induced drag.

涡流和随之产生的作用在翼型上气流的下洗流的尺寸和强度越大，诱导阻力的影响越明显。 在翼尖处，翼型上方的下洗流同样会使升力矢量向后倾斜，因此，升力垂直于相对气流向后略微倾斜，产生向后的升力分量，这就是诱导阻力。

In order to create a greater negative pressure on the top of an airfoil, the airfoil can be inclined to a higher AOA. If the AOA of a symmetrical airfoil were zero, there would be no pressure differential, and consequently, no downwash component and no induced drag. In any case, as AOA increases, induced drag increases proportionally. To state this another way—the lower the airspeed, the greater the AOA required to produce lift equal to the aircraft’s weight and, therefore, the greater induced drag. The amount of induced drag varies inversely with the square of the airspeed.

为了在翼型上表面产生更强的负压，翼型趋于产生更大的迎角。如果一个对称翼型的迎角为零， 那么不会产生压力差，导致下洗流和诱导阻力为零。在任何情况下，迎角增大，诱导阻力成比例相应增大。 换句话说——空速越小，就需要更大的迎角来产生足够的升力，使升力与重力相等，因此， 诱导阻力变大。诱导阻力与空速的平方成反比。

Conversely, parasite drag increases as the square of the airspeed. Thus, in steady state, as airspeed decreases to near the stalling speed, the total drag becomes greater, due mainly to the sharp rise in induced drag. Similarly, as the aircraft reaches its never-exceed speed (VNE), the total drag increases rapidly due to the sharp increase of parasite drag. As seen in Figure 1-3, at some given airspeed, total drag is at its minimum amount. In figuring the maximum range of aircraft, the thrust required to overcome drag is at a minimum if drag is at a minimum. The minimum power and maximum endurance occur at a different point.

相反，寄生阻力与空速的平方成正比。因此，在稳定的状态下，当空速减小到接近失速速度时， 由于诱导阻力的急剧增加，总阻力增大，当速度接近飞机的极限速度时，由于寄生阻力的急剧增大， 总阻力迅速增大。如图1-6，在某些特定空速下，总阻力处于其最小值。
