Air density
空气密度

Density is defined as the mass of an object divided by its volume, and most of our experiences with density involve solids. We know that some objects are heavier than other objects, even though they are the same size. A brick and a loaf of bread are about the same size, but a brick is heavier——it is more dense. Among metals, aluminum is less dense than iron. That's why airplanes and some automobile parts are made from aluminum.

密度被定义为物体的质量除体积， 我们接触最多的是固体的密度。我们知道，尽管一些物体的大小相同，但是有的物体比其他的物体重。一块砖和一块面包的大小相似，但砖块要重的多——砖块的密度更大。 对于金属，铝的密度比铁小，这就是为什么飞机和一些汽车的部件用铝制成。

ρ = m v

ρ：density-密度   m：mass-质量   v：volume-体积

For solids, because the molecules are bound to one another, the density remains fairly constant. But for gases, because the molecules are free to move about, density can vary over a wide range. From the kinetic theory of gases, a gas is composed of a large number of molecules that are very small relative to the distance between molecules. The molecules are in constant, random motion and frequently collide with each other and with the walls of a container. Because the molecules are in motion, a gas will expand to fill the container. Since density is defined to be the mass divided by the volume, density depends directly on the size of the container in which a gas is confined. As a simple example, consider a case on our figure. We have 26 molecules of a mythical gas. Each molecule has a mass of 20 grams (0.02 kilograms), so the mass of this gas is 0.52 kg. We have confined this gas in a rectangular tube that is 1 meter on each side and 2 meters high. The volume of the tube is 2 cubic meters, so the density is .26 kg/cubic meter. If the size of our container were decreased to 1 meter on all sides, and we kept the same number of molecules, that density would increase to 0.52 kg/cubic meter.

对于固体，因为固体分子之间连接紧密，密度几乎保持不变。但对于气体来说，因为分子可以自由移动，密度可以在一个很大的范围内变化。 根据分子运动论，气体由大量相互距离关系及其微弱的分子组成。这些分子做着持续不断的随机运动，并持续的与其他分子和容器壁相碰撞。因为分子的不断运动， 气体会膨胀填满容器。因为密度的定义是质量除体积，密度直接受容器大小的影响。举个简单的例子，想象一个场景，我们有26个未知气体分子，每个分子重20克（0.02千克） ，所以气体的质量是0.52千克。我们将气体限制在一个长和宽1米，高2米的管道内。管道的体积是2立方米，所以气体的密度是0.26千克每立方米。 如果容器的所有边都减小到1米，并保持分子的数量不变，气体的密度将会增大到0.52千克每立方米。

The density of gases is governed by the following rules:
气体密度遵循如下规律：

1. Density varies inversely with the temperature.
1. 密度与温度的大小变化相反。

As the temperature of a fluid increases it results in an increase in its volume due to the increased kinetic energy of its molecules that begin to move at higher speeds owing to which there are more frequent collisions between these molecules making them move away from each other. Now, the total mass of the gas remains the same while volume expanded, make it less dense. It is especially true when a substance is present in the liquid and gaseous form of matter due to more availability of space allowing the molecules to expand the volume of the substance conmpare to gases.

当流体的温度升高时，使分子动能增加，分子开始以更大的速度运动，使分子间的碰撞更加频繁，从而让分子间距离更远，导致流体体积增大。 现在，气体质量不变，体积增大，导致密度减小。这种变化对于液态或油状物质更为明显，因为它们相比于气体有更充足的空间来增大体积。

2. Density varies in direct proportion with the pressure.
2. 密度与压力成正比

Like in the atmosphere, air density gets greater at lower altitudes due to more air above, compressing the air below, making the same mass of air more compact, reducing in volume.

例如在大气中，由于上方空气的压缩，质量不变的空气更加紧凑，体积减小，使空气密度随高度降低而增大。

3. Density varies inversely with the humidity. The reason is stated later in the humidity section
3. 密度与湿度的大小变化相反。原因在随后的湿度内容中提到。
