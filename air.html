Air density
空气密度

Density is defined as the mass of an object divided by its volume, and most of our experiences with density involve solids. We know that some objects are heavier than other objects, even though they are the same size. A brick and a loaf of bread are about the same size, but a brick is heavier——it is more dense. Among metals, aluminum is less dense than iron. That's why airplanes and some automobile parts are made from aluminum.

密度被定义为物体的质量除体积， 我们接触最多的是固体的密度。我们知道，尽管一些物体的大小相同，但是有的物体比其他的物体重。一块砖和一块面包的大小相似，但砖块要重的多——砖块的密度更大。 对于金属，铝的密度比铁小，这就是为什么飞机和一些汽车的部件用铝制成。

ρ = m v

ρ：density-密度   m：mass-质量   v：volume-体积

For solids, because the molecules are bound to one another, the density remains fairly constant. But for gases, because the molecules are free to move about, density can vary over a wide range. From the kinetic theory of gases, a gas is composed of a large number of molecules that are very small relative to the distance between molecules. The molecules are in constant, random motion and frequently collide with each other and with the walls of a container. Because the molecules are in motion, a gas will expand to fill the container. Since density is defined to be the mass divided by the volume, density depends directly on the size of the container in which a gas is confined. As a simple example, consider a case on our figure. We have 26 molecules of a mythical gas. Each molecule has a mass of 20 grams (0.02 kilograms), so the mass of this gas is 0.52 kg. We have confined this gas in a rectangular tube that is 1 meter on each side and 2 meters high. The volume of the tube is 2 cubic meters, so the density is .26 kg/cubic meter. If the size of our container were decreased to 1 meter on all sides, and we kept the same number of molecules, that density would increase to 0.52 kg/cubic meter.

对于固体，因为固体分子之间连接紧密，密度几乎保持不变。但对于气体来说，因为分子可以自由移动，密度可以在一个很大的范围内变化。 根据分子运动论，气体由大量相互距离关系及其微弱的分子组成。这些分子做着持续不断的随机运动，并持续的与其他分子和容器壁相碰撞。因为分子的不断运动， 气体会膨胀填满容器。因为密度的定义是质量除体积，密度直接受容器大小的影响。举个简单的例子，想象一个场景，我们有26个未知气体分子，每个分子重20克（0.02千克） ，所以气体的质量是0.52千克。我们将气体限制在一个长和宽1米，高2米的管道内。管道的体积是2立方米，所以气体的密度是0.26千克每立方米。 如果容器的所有边都减小到1米，并保持分子的数量不变，气体的密度将会增大到0.52千克每立方米。

Air is a fluid
空气是流体

When most people hear the word “fluid,” they usually think of liquid. However, gasses, like air, are also fluids. Fluids take on the shape of their containers. Fluids generally do not resist deformation when even the smallest stress is applied, or they resist it only slightly. We call this slight resistance viscosity. Fluids also have the ability to flow. Just as a liquid flows and fills a container, air will expand to fill the available volume of its container. Both liquids and gasses display these unique fluid properties, even though they differ greatly in density. Understanding the fluid properties of air is essential to understanding the principles of flight.

人们在听到“流体”这个词时，往往会联想到液体。然而气体，例如空气，同样时流体。 流体的形状取决于容器的形状，就算流体被施加及其微小的压力，它们也不会抵抗变形，或及其微弱的抵抗变形。 我们称这种微弱的抵抗为粘性。流体会流动，就像液体流入容器并将容器填满一样，气体会膨胀填满容器内的可用空间。 尽管液体和气体的密度相差巨大，但它们都表现出这种独特的流体特性。理解流体的特性对于飞行原理的学习至关重要。

Viscosity
粘性

Viscosity is the property of a fluid that causes it to resist flowing. Because part of a fluid that is forced to move carries along to some extent adjacent parts, viscosity may be thought of as internal friction between the molecules. The way individual molecules of the fluid tend to adhere, or stick, to each other determines how much a fluid resists flow. High-viscosity fluids are “thick” and resist flow; low-viscosity fluids are “thin” and flow easily. Air has a low viscosity and flows easily.

粘性是流体抑制自身流动的一种特性。因为流体的一部分被迫流动时，在一定程度上带着附近的一部分流体流动，粘性可以理解为分子之间的摩擦力。流体分子相互吸引，或相互黏着的程度，决定了流体阻碍自身流动的程度。 高粘性的流体更加的“稠”并阻碍流动，低粘性的流体更加的“稀”并容易流动。空气的粘性很低，流动容易。

Using two liquids as an example, similar amounts of oil and water poured down two identical ramps will flow at different rates due to their different viscosity. The water seems to flow freely while the oil flows much more slowly.

以两种液体为例，量相同的油和水分别倒入两个相同的斜坡，因为它们的粘性不同，流动速度也就不同。 水几乎是自由流动，而油相比要慢上许多。

All fluids are viscous and have a resistance to flow, whether or not we observe this resistance. We cannot easily observe the viscosity of air. However, since air is a fluid and has viscosity properties, it resists flow around any object to some extent.

无论我们是否能观察到这种阻碍，所有的流体都具有粘性并具有阻碍流动的特性。我们无法简单的就能观测到空气的粘性。 然而，因为空气是流体，具有粘性，在一定程度上，它会阻碍自身围绕物体的流动。

Friction
摩擦力

Another factor at work when a fluid flows over or around an object is called friction. Friction is the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another. Friction exists between any two materials that contact each other.

当流体流过或围绕物体流动时的另一个影响因素是摩擦力。摩擦力是将一个表面或物体在 另一个表面或物体上移动时遇到的阻碍。摩擦力存在于任意两个相互接触的物质。

The effects of friction can be demonstrated using a similar example as before. If identical fluids are poured down two identical ramps, they flow in the same manner and at the same speed. If the surface of one ramp is rough, and the other smooth, the flow down the two ramps differs significantly. The rough surface ramp impedes the flow of the fluid due to resistance from the surface (friction). It is important to remember that all surfaces, no matter how smooth they appear, are not smooth on a microscopic level and impede the flow of a fluid.

摩擦力的影响可用与上文中相似的例子来展示。 如果将相同的流体以相同的速度倒向相同的两个斜坡，如果其中一个斜坡的表面粗糙，另一个斜坡表面光滑， 那么两个斜坡的流动会有显著不同。表面粗糙的斜坡由于表面的阻碍（摩擦力），阻碍流体的流动。 一定要记住，所有的表面，无论看起来有多光滑，在显微镜下都不是光滑的，会阻碍流体的流动。

The surface of a wing, like any other surface, has a certain roughness at the microscopic level. The surface roughness causes resistance and slows the velocity of the air flowing over the wing. [Figure 1-1]

机翼的表面，就像其他表面一样，在显微镜下能看到具体的粗糙程度。粗糙表面产生阻碍，减慢空气流过机翼的速度。

Pressure
压力

Pressure is the force applied in a perpendicular direction to the surface of an object. Often, pressure is measured in pounds of force exerted per square inch of an object——PSI(America). There are also some other ways of measuring pressure, we will talk about that later. An object completely immersed in a fluid will feel pressure uniformly around the entire surface of the object. If the pressure on one surface of the object becomes less than the pressure exerted on the other surfaces, the object will move in the direction of the lower pressure.

压力是垂直于物体表面施加的力，通常，压力用施加在物体每“平方英寸”上的压力“磅”——PSI表示（美制单位）。还有其他表示压力的单位，我们将会在之后提到。 一个完全浸入流体的物体的所有表面将会一致地承受压力。如果一侧表面的压力变得小于另一侧的压力， 物体会朝着低压的方向运动。
