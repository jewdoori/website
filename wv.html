Water Vapor
水汽

Water vapor is the gaseous form of water and one of the most important of all constituents of the atmosphere. It constitutes only a small percentage of the Earth’s atmosphere, varying from only trace amounts to 4 percent by volume, and its amount varies widely in space and time. Approximately half of all of the atmospheric water vapor is found below 2 km (6,500 ft) altitude, and only a minute fraction of the total occurs above the tropopause.

水汽是水的气体形态，是大气的重要组成部分。水汽只占地球大气的一小部分，含量在痕量到4%之间，而且其含量 随地点和时间的变化而差异显著。整个大气半数的水汽都聚集在2km（6500ft）高度以下，处于对流层之上的 水汽只占很小一部分。

The development of clouds and precipitation can have potential impacts on flight operations. Water vapor is important, not only as the raw material for clouds and precipitation (e.g., rain and snow), but also as a vehicle for the transfer of heat energy and as a regulator of the Earth’s temperatures through absorption and emission of radiation, most significantly in the thermal infrared. The amount of water vapor present in a given air sample may be measured in a number of different ways, involving such concepts as relative humidity and dewpoint. Before talking about these subjects, change of phase and how water cycles through the Earth-atmosphere system will be discussed.

云和降水的产生会间接影响飞行任务。水汽不仅是产生云和降水（例如雨，雪等等）的原材料，也是热能传递的载体和地球温度的调节器。 调节温度是通过吸收辐射和释放辐射来完成的，尤其是通过红外线。在一个给定的空气样本中，水汽的含量可用多种方式测量， 涉及相对湿度和露点等概念。在讲述这些课题之前，先讲讲相变和地球大气系统中水的循环过程。

Change of Phase
相变

Water changes from one state of matter (e.g., solid, liquid, or vapor) to another at the temperatures and pressures experienced near the surface of the Earth. Interestingly, water is the only substance on Earth that exists naturally in all three phases: as water droplets, ice crystals (visible as clouds), and water vapor.

地表附近在温度和气压的变化下，水从物质的一个状态（例如，固态，液态，气态）转变为另一个状态。 有趣的是，水是地球上唯一一种在自然中三种形态都存在的物质：水滴，冰晶（云），水汽。

Water has some unique thermal properties, which make it a powerful heat transport mechanism. It has the highest specific heat capacity of any naturally occurring substance (see Table 5-4). That means water has a much higher capacity for storing heat energy (with little resulting temperature change) than other substances. These properties make water an ideal heat transport mechanism and have important implications on weather and climate.

水具有独特的热属性，这使它成为一种强大的热交换工具。水在所有自然存在的物质之中，具有最高的比热容（见表格1-1）。 这意味比起其他物质，水具有的容纳热能的能力（容纳热能导致的温度变化小）更高。这些性质使得水成为一种理想的热传递 方式，对于气象和气候的影响极其重大。

Latent Heat
潜热

Latent heat is the quantity of heat energy either released or absorbed by a unit mass of a substance when it undergoes a phase transition. Units are typically expressed in terms of joules per gram (J/g). Figure 1-2 illustrates the latent heat transactions that occur when water undergoes phase transition.

潜热是单位质量的物质在相变过程中，释放或吸收的热能的量。单位通常用焦耳每克（J/g）表示。如图1-2 所示，水在进行相变时潜热的变化。

图1-2

Heat is exchanged between water and its environment during phase transition. Although the temperature of the environment changes in response, the temperature of the water undergoing the phase transition remains constant until the phase change is complete; that is, the latent heat, is involved exclusively in changing the phase of water and not in changing its temperature. There are six phase transitions, three of which are associated with the absorption of latent heat by water from the environment (melting, evaporation, and sublimation), and three of which are associated with the release of heat energy by water to the environment (freezing, condensation, and deposition).

在相变过程中，水与环境进行热交换，尽管在这一过程中环境温度持续变化，但直到相变完成之前，处在相变过程中的水的温度将保持不变。 这便是潜热，它只参与水的相变，而不改变其温度。相变有六种，其中三种是从环境中吸收潜热（融化，蒸发和升华）， 另三种是释放热能到环境当中（凝固，凝聚，凝华）。

1.Evaporation
  蒸发

Evaporation is the phase transition by which a liquid is changed to a vapor (gas). In meteorology, the substance meteorologists are concerned about the most is water, and the primary source is the ocean. On average, about 120 cm (47 in) is evaporated into the atmosphere from the ocean each year. For evaporation to take place, energy is required. The energy can come from any source: the Sun, the atmosphere, the Earth, or objects on the Earth, such as humans.

蒸发是液体转变成蒸汽（气体）的相变过程。在气象学中，气象学家最关心的物质就是水，水是海洋的基础构成物质。 海洋每年平均大约蒸发120厘米（47英尺）深的海水进入大气。蒸发需要能量。能量有多种来源：太阳，大气，地球， 或地球上的物体，比如人类。

Everyone has experienced evaporation personally. When the body heats up due to the air temperature or through exercise, the body sweats, secreting water onto the skin. The purpose is to cause the body to use its heat to evaporate the liquid, thereby removing heat and cooling the body. The same effect can be seen when stepping out of a shower or swimming pool. The coolness felt is from the removal of bodily heat used to evaporate the water on the skin.

所有人都能感受蒸发这一过程。当由于气温或者健身，身体变热出汗，分泌水分到皮肤表面。 其目的便是使身体利用热量去蒸发液体，从而达到使身体消散热量并冷却的目的。在离开澡堂或泳池 时也能观察到同样的现象，感到寒冷的感觉便是由于体表水分挥发使身体散热。

2.Transpiration
  蒸腾

Transpiration is the evaporation of water from plants. In most plants, transpiration is a passive process largely controlled by the humidity of the atmosphere and the moisture content of the soil. Of the transpired water passing through a plant, only 1 percent is used in the growth process of the plant. The remaining 99 percent is passed into the atmosphere.

蒸腾是水从植物中蒸发的过程。对于大多数植物，蒸腾是一个被动的过程，其主要由大气湿度和土壤湿度掌控。 当水通过蒸腾流过整个植物后，只有百分之1的水被用于植物生长，其余的百分之99进入了大气之中。

3.Sublimation
  升华

Sublimation is the phase transition by which a solid is changed into vapor (a gas) without passing through the liquid phase. In the atmosphere, sublimation of water occurs when ice and snow (solids) change into water vapor (a gas).

升华是固体转化成蒸汽（气体）的相变过程。在大气中，水的升华发生在雪（固体）转化成水蒸气（气体）的过程。

4.Deposition
  凝华

Deposition is the phase transition by which vapor (a gas) is changed into a solid without passing through the liquid phase. In the atmosphere, deposition of water occurs when vapor (a gas) in a sub-freezing cloud changes into ice crystals (solid).

凝华是蒸汽（气体）不经过液相直接转化成固体的相变过程。在大气中，水的凝华发生在水汽（气体）在低于零度的云中转变成冰晶（固体）。

5.Condensation
  凝聚

Condensation is the phase transition by which vapor (a gas) is changed into a liquid. In the atmosphere, condensation may appear as clouds, fog, dew, or frost, depending upon the physical conditions of the atmosphere.

凝聚是蒸汽（气体）转变成液体的相变过程。凝聚会根据大气的物理条件，以云、雾、露、霜的形式出现。

6.Transportation
  输送

Transportation is the movement of solid, liquid, and gaseous water through the atmosphere. Without this movement, the water evaporated over the ocean would not precipitate over land.

输送是固体，液体和气体形态的水在大气的运动。如果没有这种运动，海上蒸发的水汽无法在陆地形成降水。

7.Precipitation
  降水

Precipitation results when tiny condensation particles grow in the atmosphere through collision and coalescence and then fall to the Earth’s surface.

降水是由大气中凝聚的微小水滴通过碰撞合并然后降落在地表形成的。

8.Runoff
  径流

Runoff occurs when there is excessive precipitation and the ground is saturated (i.e., cannot absorb any more water). This runoff flows into streams and rivers and eventually back into the sea.

当降水过多且地面已经饱和时（无法吸收更多的水分），径流出现。径流会汇入溪流和河流中并最终流回大海。

9.Infiltration
  渗透

Infiltration is the movement of water into the ground from the surface.

渗透是水从地表进入地面的过程。

10.Groundwater Flow
   地下水流

Groundwater flow is the flow of water underground in aquifers. The water may return to the surface in springs or eventually seep into the oceans.

地下水流是指在地下含水层中水的流动。这些水可能会以喷泉的形式返回地表或是渗入海洋。

11.Plant Uptake
   植物吸收

Plant uptake is water taken from the groundwater flow and soil moisture.

植物吸收是指植物吸收地下水或是土壤水分。

Saturation
饱和

Saturation is the maximum possible quantity of water vapor that an air parcel can hold at any given temperature and pressure. The term “saturated air” means an air parcel has all of the water vapor it can hold, while “unsaturated air” means an air parcel can hold more water vapor.

饱和是指在任意给定的温度和气压下，一个气团可以容纳的最大水汽量。“饱和空气”指的是气团已经容纳了它所能容纳的所有水汽。 “未饱和空气”则指气团还能容纳更多水汽。

Relative Humidity
相对湿度

Relative humidity is the ratio, usually expressed as a percentage, of water vapor actually in the air parcel compared to the amount of water vapor the air parcel could hold at a particular temperature and pressure.

相对湿度是一个比值，通常用百分比表示。相对湿度是在特定的温度和气压下，空气中实际的水汽量与空气可容纳的水汽含量的比。

Relative Humidity = Water vapor content Water vapor capacity

While relative humidity is the most common method of describing atmospheric moisture, it is also the most misunderstood. Relative humidity can be confusing because it does not indicate the actual water vapor content of the air, but rather how close the air is to saturation. An air parcel with 100 percent relative humidity is saturated, while an air parcel with relative humidity less than 100 percent is unsaturated.

相对湿度是描述大气水分的最常用的方式，它也最容易被误解。其迷惑性在于它不表示空气中实际的水汽含量， 而是表示空气距离达到饱和的程度。相对湿度为100%的气团是饱和的，相对湿度低于100%是不饱和的。

An air parcel’s capacity to hold water vapor (at a constant pressure) is directly related to its temperature. It is possible to change an air parcel’s relative humidity without changing its water vapor content. Table 3-1 . An air parcel at sea level at a temperature of 30 °C has the capacity to hold 27 g of water vapor. If it actually held 8 g, its relative humidity would be 30 percent, and it would be unsaturated. However, if the air parcel’s temperature decreases to 20 °C, its water vapor storage capacity decreases to 15 g and its relative humidity rises to 53 percent. At 10 °C, the air parcel’s water vapor storage capacity decreases to equal the amount of water vapor it actually holds (8 g), its relative humidity increases to 100 percent, and it becomes saturated. During this cooling process, the air parcel’s actual water vapor content remained constant, but relative humidity increased with decreasing temperature.

气团容纳水汽的能力（在一定气压下）直接受到其温度的影响。可以无需改变一个气团的水汽含量 就可以改变它的相对湿度。表格3-1，一个在海平面的气团，温度为30°C，可以容纳27克水汽。 如果它实际容纳了8克水汽，那么他的相对湿度便是30%，它是未饱和的。然而，如果气团温度降低至20°C， 他可容纳的的水汽量降低到15克，那么它的相对湿度升高到53%。在10°C时，气团的水汽容纳量降低至于其 实际容纳的水汽量相等（8克），气团的相对湿度上升到100%，处于饱和状态。在降温过程中，气团的水汽含量不变， 但相对湿度随温度降低而升高。

Dew point
露点

Dewpoint is the temperature an air parcel must be cooled at constant pressure and constant water vapor pressure to allow the water vapor in the parcel to condense into water (dew). When this temperature is below 0 °C (32 °F), it is sometimes called the frost point. Lowering an air parcel’s temperature reduces its capacity to hold water vapor.

气团在一定的气压和水汽压下，温度降低使水汽凝聚成水（露），此时的温度叫露点。当露点低于0°C（32°F）时， 它有时也被称作霜点。气团的温度降低会导致其容纳水汽的能力下降。

Temperature-Dewpoint Spread (Dewpoint Depression)
温度露点差 （露点差）

The difference between an air parcel’s temperature and its dewpoint is the dewpoint depression, or commonly referred to as the spread. Surface aviation weather reports (e.g., METARs/SPECIs) provide observations of both temperature and dewpoint. The temperature greatly affects the air parcel’s ability to hold water vapor, while the dewpoint indicates the actual quantity of water vapor in the parcel. As the spread decreases, relative humidity increases. When the spread decreases to zero, relative humidity is 100 percent, and the air parcel is saturated. Figure 3-2 illustrates the relationship between temperature-dewpoint spread and relative humidity.

气团的温度和露点的差值叫做露点差，或者称作温度露点差。低空民航气象报（例如，机场例行天气报告/机场特殊天气报告） 中会提供温度和露点。气团容纳水汽的能力极大地受温度的影响。露点表示气团中实际的水汽含量。当温度露点差减小时， 相对湿度增大。当温度露点差降低至零时，相对湿度为100%，此时气团是饱和的。图3-2示温度露点差与相对湿度的关系。

Surface temperature-dewpoint spread is important in anticipating fog, but has little bearing on precipitation. To support precipitation, air must be saturated through thick layers aloft.

地表温度露点差在雾的预测中起到重要作用，但在预测降水时作用很小。必须是在高空中较厚的空气层中的空气达到饱和，才能形成降水。

Relative humidity depends on the temperature-dewpoint spread. In Figure 3-2, dewpoint is constant but temperature decreases from left to right. On the left panel, relative humidity (RH) is 50 percent, which indicates the air parcel could hold twice as much water vapor as is actually present. As the air parcel cools, the temperature-dewpoint spread decreases while relative humidity increases. When the air parcel’s temperature cools to equal its dewpoint (11 °C), its capacity to hold water vapor is reduced to the amount actually present. The temperature-dewpoint spread is zero, relative humidity is 100 percent, and the air parcel is now saturated.
